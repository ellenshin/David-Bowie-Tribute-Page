Here is a timeline of David Bowie's life:

  • 1947: David Bowie is born David Robert Hayward-Jones in Brixton, London.
  • 1962: Bowie forms his first band, the Konrads.
  • 1967: Bowie releases his self-titled debut album, which (along with its first singles), fails to chart upon its initial release.
  • 1970: Bowie releases The Man Who Sold the World, his first fully realized album, which transitioned the British folk of his earlier releases into a heavier rock sound, and also saw the singer playing with the androgynous image he'd help pioneer.
  • 1972: Bowie releases his landmark record The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars, a concept album that saw Bowie embracing a flamboyant glam-rock frontman status and cemented his popularity in the UK.
  • 1976: Bowie stars in the sci-fi film The Man Who Fell to Earth, playing an alien who journeys to Earth to save his extraterrestrial home planet. Loosely based on his role, Bowie debuts a new personality The White Duke, coinciding with the release of his album Station to Station.
  • 1977-1979: Bowie releases the acclaimed "Berlin trilogy" of albums, recorded during his time in Germany: the Krautrock-influenced Low and Heroes (both released in 1977) and the more pop-oriented Lodger (1979).
  • 1984: Bowie gets his first Grammy, winning the Best Short Form Music Video category for his 20-minute film Jazzin' for Blue Jean, made to promote the single Blue Jean from his Tonight album.
  • 1996: Bowie is inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 2006: Bowie is honored with the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award.
  • 2013: On his 66th birthday, Bowie announces the release of The Next Day, his first studio album in a decade.
  • 2016: Three years later, on his 69th birthday, Bowie releases his 25th and final studio album, Blackstar.
  • 2016: Bowie dies at the age of 69, after a battle with cancer kept secret from the public.
